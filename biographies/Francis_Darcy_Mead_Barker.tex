\biohead{Francis Darcy Mead Barker}{}

Francis Darcy Mead Barker (also known as Frank)  was born April-May-June 1880 in Liverpool, Lancashire to Thomas Henry Barker \bioref{Thomas_Henry_Barker} and Mary Ellen Moulsdale {Mary_Ellen_Moulsdale}  \cite{FBarkerBirth}. He had six siblings: James Denton Barker \bioref{James_Denton_Barker}, Charles Frederick Strangeways Barker \bioref{Charles_Frederick_Strangeways_Barker}, Reverend Thomas Percy Conyers Barker \bioref{Thomas_Percy_Conyers_Barker}, William Danby Holt Barker \bioref{William_Danby_Holt_Barker}, Jonathan Tong Barker \bioref{Jonathan_Tong_Barker} and Henry Bertram Mitford Barker \bioref{Henry_Bertram_Mitford_Barker}.

He married Isabel Whitehead \bioref{Isabel_Whitehead} on 19 May 1910 in the Wirral, Cheshire \cite{FBarkerMarriage} and they had one son, John Darcy Barker. \bioref{John_Darcy_Barker}.
He served in the Merchant Navy during World War One, but there is no record of his actual position;  he was granted Campaign Medals for his service. \cite{FDMBarkerMedals}
He was a Ship's Purser on a steam ship (the name of which is not legible in the census record). \cite{FBarkerOccupation}

He died on 8 January 1937 in	Neston, Cheshire \cite{FBarkerDeath} and the Probate notice read:
``Barker Francis Darcy Mead of Mendi Parkgate Cheshire died 8 January 1937 at Neston Cheshire.
Administration Liverpool 5 February to Isabel Barker widow effects \pounds 731.12s.''
